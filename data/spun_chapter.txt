## The Gates of Morning, Book 1, Chapter 1: The Canoe Builder

Dick stood on a coral ledge, his gaze fixed on the southern horizon.  Behind him, the outer sea roared, its spray scattered by the wind. Before him lay the lagoon of Karolin, calm as a lake—an immense expanse of blue, alive with circling gulls.

Enclosed by a forty-mile coral reef, this vast lagoon was a sea unto itself; tempestuous in strong winds, a tranquil azure lake in gentler breezes. And it was his—his since his arrival just yesterday.

Along the beach, bathed in the blazing sun, the tribe bustled: women, children, and youths fishing, playing, or working on their *paraka* patches.  They were his people. The canoes drawn up on the sand, the empty houses that once held war canoes—all were his.

His gaze shifted from the lagoon to the canoe houses. His brow furrowed. Turning his back on the lagoon, he faced the breakers of the northern sea.  Beyond the horizon, unseen, lay Palm Tree Island, a place of breathtaking beauty…
